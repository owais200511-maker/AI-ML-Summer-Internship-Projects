Samsung Washing Machine User Manual & Troubleshooting Guide

Washing Cycles and Modes

Normal Cycle: Used for standard cottons, bed linens, table linens, undergarments, and shirts. Optimal load size is 6.0 kg. Uses warm water and medium spin speed.

Delicates Cycle: Recommended for sheer fabrics, bras, lingerie, silks, and hand-wash-only garments. Uses cold water, low spin speed, and gentle agitation to prevent damage.

Quick Wash Cycle: A 15-minute quick wash mode for lightly soiled garments under 2.0 kg. Uses cold or warm water and high spin speed.

Heavy Duty Cycle: Designed for heavily soiled, colorfast garments like work clothes, jeans, and towels. Uses hot water and high spin speed.

Error Codes and Troubleshooting

  • Code 4C or 4E: Water Supply Error. Check that the water taps are open and that the inlet hoses are not kinked. Clean the mesh filters inside the inlet valves.
  • Code 5C or 5E: Drainage Error. Inspect the drain filter at the bottom right front of the machine for blockages. Ensure the drain hose is not clogged or twisted.
  • Code dC or dE: Door/Lid Open Error. Ensure the door is fully closed and the latch is engaged. If error persists, check the door switch.
  • Code Ub or UE: Unbalanced Load Error. The laundry load is unevenly distributed. Pause the cycle, rearrange the items to distribute weight evenly, and restart the spin cycle.

Maintenance Tips

Self Clean / Tub Clean Mode: Run this cycle once a month without laundry or detergent to clean the drum and remove bacterial residue. Add liquid chlorine bleach to the detergent compartment for best results.

Emergency Drain: In case of drainage failure, open the filter cover at the bottom right, extract the emergency drain tube, remove the plug to drain remaining water, and then unscrew the filter for cleaning.
